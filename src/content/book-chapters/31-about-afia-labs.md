Afia Labs is where I build products that make everyday life feel lighter, smarter, and a little more human.

Some of what I build is practical. Some of it is playful. Some of it is for adults trying to get their digital lives together. Some of it is for kids learning about the world. But all of it comes from the same belief: technology should help people feel more capable, more in control, and more like themselves.

Right now, that includes MemeScanr, a playful iPhone photo cleanup app that makes storage cleanup feel less like a chore and more like a reset. It also includes Planet Wonders, a kids app designed to make learning feel creative, magical, and alive. And it includes TripSquad, a group travel planning idea built around a very real problem: getting people to actually agree.

The products are different on purpose. The foundation is the same.

I care about three things when I build:

Ownership first.
I want the people using my products to feel more in control, not less. More clarity. More confidence. Less digital chaos.

Personality matters.
I do not believe software has to feel cold to be useful. Voice is not extra. It is part of the experience.

Useful should still feel good.
A product can solve a real problem and still feel warm, thoughtful, fun, or memorable. In fact, I think the best ones do.

You can find Afia Labs at afialabs.net.
